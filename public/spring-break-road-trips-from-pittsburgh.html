Pittsburgh's spring arrives slowly, with gray skies and chilly rain well into April. Fortunately, warmer destinations are a straight shot south through the mountains. Best Spring Break Destinations Within Driving Distance

  • Asheville, North Carolina

    Asheville is about 7 hours from Pittsburgh through the scenic Appalachian corridor. Spring temperatures reach the low 60s, and the city's craft breweries, galleries, and farm-to-table restaurants make it a favorite. One of the best spring break road trips from Pittsburgh for mountain lovers.

  • Nashville, Tennessee

    Roughly 8 hours from Pittsburgh, Nashville warms into the mid-60s for spring break. The music scene, hot chicken, and walkable downtown give you plenty to fill a week. It is a dependable warm-weather destination that does not require an extreme drive.

  • Charleston, South Carolina

    Charleston sits about 8 hours south of Pittsburgh and offers spring highs in the low 70s. The historic district, carriage rides, and waterfront restaurants create a warm-weather escape that feels worlds away from western Pennsylvania. A top warm place to drive for spring break.

  • Outer Banks, North Carolina

    Around 8 hours southeast, the Outer Banks delivers quiet spring beaches with temperatures in the low 60s. Off-season means fewer crowds, lower prices, and miles of open coastline. Lighthouse tours and fresh seafood round out a relaxing spring break trip.

  • Savannah, Georgia

    Savannah is roughly 10 hours from Pittsburgh, but mid-70s temperatures and moss-covered squares make the drive easy to justify. The riverfront, historic homes, and Southern cooking deliver one of the warmest road trips within driving distance of Pittsburgh during spring break.
