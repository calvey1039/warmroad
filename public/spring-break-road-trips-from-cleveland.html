Cleveland springs are cold and gray, and waiting for warm weather takes patience most people don't have by March. The good news is that warmer destinations are closer than you think. From the Appalachians to the Carolina coast, there are excellent warm road trips within driving distance of Cleveland.

Best Spring Break Destinations Within Driving Distance

  • Asheville, North Carolina

    Asheville is about 7 hours south of Cleveland through the mountains. Spring brings temperatures in the low 60s, craft breweries are everywhere, and the Blue Ridge Parkway starts greening up. It is one of the best spring break road trips from Cleveland for anyone who likes mountain towns with character.

  • Gatlinburg, Tennessee

    Around 7 hours from Cleveland, Gatlinburg offers direct access to the Smokies. Spring wildflower season draws hikers, and temperatures settle into the upper 50s to low 60s. The town itself has plenty of restaurants and shops to fill your evenings after a day on the trails.

  • Charleston, South Carolina

    Charleston is roughly 9 hours from Cleveland, and spring break temperatures in the low 70s make the drive worthwhile. Walk the historic district, eat fresh seafood along the waterfront, and enjoy warm evenings on King Street. A top choice among warm places to drive for spring break.

  • Outer Banks, North Carolina

    About 9 hours southeast of Cleveland, the Outer Banks offers uncrowded beaches during spring break with temperatures around 60 to 65°F. It is perfect for quiet beach walks, lighthouse tours, and fresh seafood. The shoulder season pricing makes it even more appealing.

  • Savannah, Georgia

    Savannah sits roughly 10 hours south of Cleveland, where spring highs reach the mid-70s. Moss-draped squares, Southern cooking, and the historic riverfront district give you plenty of reasons to make the trip. One of the warmest road trips within driving distance from Cleveland.
