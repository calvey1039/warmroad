Best Spring Break Destinations Within Driving Distance

  • Nashville, Tennessee

    Nashville sits about 7 hours south of Chicago and averages highs in the mid-60s during March. The city offers live music on Broadway, outstanding food, and warm enough weather to walk the Gulch and East Nashville neighborhoods without a heavy coat. A solid pick for spring break road trips from Chicago.

  • Gatlinburg, Tennessee

    Roughly 8 hours from Chicago, Gatlinburg is the gateway to Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Spring brings wildflower blooms and temperatures in the upper 50s to low 60s. It is a great base for hiking and enjoying mountain views while escaping the Midwest freeze.

  • Hot Springs, Arkansas

    About 9 hours southwest of Chicago, Hot Springs pairs natural thermal baths with a charming downtown. Spring temperatures reach the low 70s, making it one of the warmer places to drive for spring break. Bathhouse Row and the surrounding Ouachita National Forest are worth the trip.

  • Dallas, Texas

    Dallas is roughly 13 hours from Chicago, but spring break temperatures in the mid-70s make it worthwhile. The Deep Ellum district, Fort Worth Stockyards, and Texas BBQ scene give you plenty to do. Split the drive over two days and enjoy the warm weather all week.

  • Austin, Texas

    At about 15 hours from Chicago, Austin rewards the longer drive with highs near 80°F during spring break. South Congress Avenue, Barton Springs, and the live music scene make Austin one of the best warm road trips within driving distance for anyone willing to push a bit farther south.
